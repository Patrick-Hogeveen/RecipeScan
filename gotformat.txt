CEDAR PLANKED SALMON WITH SPICE RUB
1 cedar plank (approx. 8 " by \(12 " / 22 \mathrm{~cm}\) by \(30 \mathrm{~cm}\) )
Use untreated cedar available at lumber yards or hardware stores
\(2 \mathrm{lb}(1 \mathrm{~kg}) \quad\) salmon fillet, with skin
\(1 \mathrm{Tbsp}(15 \mathrm{ml}) \quad\) olive oil
\(3 \mathrm{Tbsp}(45 \mathrm{ml}) \quad\) brown sugar
\(1 \mathrm{Tbsp}(15 \mathrm{ml}) \quad\) paprika
\(1 \mathrm{Tbsp}(15 \mathrm{ml}) \quad\) salt
\(1 \mathrm{Tbsp}(15 \mathrm{ml}) \quad\) pepper
\(1 / 2 \mathrm{tsp}(2 \mathrm{ml}) \quad\) ground cumin
1. Soak plank in a tub of cold water for at least 1 hour and up to 12 hours.
2. Score top side of salmon in about \(2^{\prime \prime} / 5 \mathrm{~cm}\) diagonal slits, but do not cut too deeply or through to the flesh.
3. Rub olive oil into salmon.
4. In a small bowl, combine sugar, paprika, salt, black pepper and cumin. Rub all over salmon and into slits. Marinate salmon 10 to 20 minutes.
5. Place salmon on soaked plank. Put the plank and salmon, on the rack of a preheated barbecue.
6. Close the lid and barbecue on medium-high heat 25 to 30 minutes or until salmon is cooked through. Do not turn fish during cooking.
7. The fish can be brought to the table on the plank decorated with fresh herbs or the salmon can be transferred with a wide lifter to a serving platter or a clean plank.
Makes 6 servings
(Bonnie Stern recipe taken from National Post, Aug 12/2000)
